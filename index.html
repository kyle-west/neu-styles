Neumorphism

Levels

Neumorphic Design relies on elements living in 3 main levels. Like the levels of a 2-story house, elements reside on the main floor, the top floor, or in the basement.

Interaction

When an element is interacted with, it transitions toward the main floor, but refrains from entering that level. For example, buttons simulate the pressing of a physical button. They should contain bright/strong colors to attract the user's attention.


The depression of a text input directs the user to "enter" that space.

Checkboxes and radio buttons are also depressed, but they remain at a shallow inset because of their small size.
